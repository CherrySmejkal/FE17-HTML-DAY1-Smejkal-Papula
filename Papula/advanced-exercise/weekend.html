Saturday Old Viennese Café breakfast: There’s also something to be said about this quote from the Dutch writer, Geert Mak – ‘Without coffeehouse there is no Vienna. Without Vienna, there is no coffeehouses.’ This is so true. The city’s soul has lived, and been brought to life and hotly discussed, in these holy places that serve coffee for many, many years. This would partly explain why their timeless charm survives today, making them just as beloved as they were when famous artists that are now printed on t-shirts once sat in their booths. Sunday
  1. Sunday Brunch
    • Brunch has become the most important meal of the day for millennials on holiday and Austria has been ahead of the game for years.
    • One of the best places to go for a brunch is Palmenhaus.
    • Adresse: Burggarten 1, 1010 Wien
  2. Visit Naschmarkt
    • The Naschmarkt is Vienna's most popular market. Located at the Wienzeile over the Wien River, it is about 1.5 kilometres (0.93 mi) long.
    • The Naschmarkt has existed since the 16th century.Nowadays, one can buy fresh fruit and vegetables from around the world, exotic herbs, cheese, baked goods such as bread, kaiser rolls, torte, meats, and seafood. There are also many small restaurants.
  3. Jogging in Prater
    • With 600 hectares, the Prater is about twice the size of Hampstead Heath and the third-biggest park in Vienna.
    • The main running route is the Hauptallee, a dead straight, car-free, 4.4km (2.7 miles) road through the park that’s perfect for tempo and interval training.
  1. Stöckl im Park
    • If you like being in nature, the Stöckl is the right place for you. The ambience of the restaurant welcomes you with a lot of friendly, light wood – and the large panoramic windows allow a view of the surrounding green and the historic park from all sides. A place to relax and linger. The menu includes Viennese classics such as boiled beef, Schnitzel or black pudding as well as regional fish specialties and vegetarian delicacies.
    • Our in-house, ultra-modern brewery is located right next to the restaurant. ll of our beers are brewed on site, are unfiltered and not pasteurized. And that’s exactly how they taste: wonderfully fresh and natural.
    • Address: Prinz Eugen-Straße 25, 1030 Wien
  2. Visit Wiener Therme
    • The Therme Wien thermal baths offer a haven of relaxation, preventive healthcare treatments and spa therapies – all in one place. Treat yourself to some time out and rediscover your own pace while you relax and connect with your inner self.
    • Relax in the generously dimensioned pools, arranged like giant rocks charting the course of an imaginary river bed. Choose between the Beauty Stone, the Stone of Tranquillity, the Adventure Stone, the Sauna Stone with its Solewelt salt world and, finally, the Fitness Stone.
    • Address: Kurbadstraße 14, 1100 Wien
  3. Meeting Place MuseumsQuartier
    • The MuseumsQuartier (MQ) is one of the largest cultural quarters in the world. Located at the border of the old city in the former imperial stables, it combines institutions of different art fields, restaurants, cafés and shops in an area of over 60,000 square feet in a post-modern ambiance, a combination of baroque buildings and modern architecture.
    • The MQ offers an ambiance that fits the urban lifestyle of its visitors: retaining the old, experiencing the new – and enjoying both of them together.
    • Address: Museumsplatz 1/5 1070 Wien
